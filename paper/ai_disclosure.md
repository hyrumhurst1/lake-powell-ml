# AI-Assistance Disclosure

Most journals now ask for this, and stating it plainly protects your credibility
rather than threatening it. Adapt the wording to the venue's exact policy.

---

**Statement for the manuscript:**

> The author used AI-based tools (large language model coding and writing
> assistants) to scaffold analysis code, to draft and edit prose, and to organize
> references. All study design choices, methodological decisions, execution and
> checking of the analysis, interpretation of results, and final wording were
> made and verified by the author. The author takes full responsibility for the
> accuracy and integrity of the work. No AI tool is listed as an author.

---

**Why this is the right call:**
- Honesty here is now standard practice, not a red flag. Undisclosed AID use that
  is later discovered is the thing that actually destroys credibility.
- It is true. You did design it, run it, check it, and you can defend it.
- It models exactly the "researcher who is also fluent in applied AI tooling"
  position you want. Using the tools well and transparently is a strength.

**What you must be able to back up:** that the design, interpretation, and claims
are genuinely yours. That is what the defense brief is for.
